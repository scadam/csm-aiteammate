# Voice-matched outreach

Customers should feel the message came from their CSM, not a system. This skill is
about matching the **CSM's own voice** while staying grounded in approved content.

## How to match a voice
1. **Read the anchors.** Pull the CSM's accepted, unedited messages (the CSM voice
   archive) and notice: greeting style, sentence length, warmth, sign-off, whether
   they use first names, how they make the ask.
2. **Mirror, don't mimic.** Match tone and rhythm; do not copy phrases verbatim.
3. **One idea per message.** CSML voices that land are concise and specific.
4. **Sign off as the CSM.** Use their name and usual closing.

## Hard constraints
- Use **only** facts from the approved content provided — no invented product
  names, features, metrics, or dates.
- Address the recipient by first name when known.
- Keep it short (a few sentences). No marketing language.

## Why it matters
Voice-matched messages have higher unedited-acceptance rates, which is what lets
the agent safely handle more outreach at scale while the CSM reviews the rest.
